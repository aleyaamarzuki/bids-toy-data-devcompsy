Thank you for taking part in the study!

This study aimed to replicate a psychological effect called Illusory Correlation (Hamilton and Gifford, 1976). In the illusory correlation person characteristics probabilistically occur with both majority and minority groups. This means that there was no correlation between characteristics and group membership. You learned about these relationships during the first phase, then you were transitioned to the test phase. In the test phase, if the rarely occurring characteristic is judged to be representative of the minority group and the frequently occurring feature to be representative of the majority group, then we observe the illusory correlation.
