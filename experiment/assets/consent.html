Grouping Task!

In this online study you will be asked to complete two phases: one learning phase and one testing phase. In the learning phase you will be shown statements describing students by a single characteristic and their group membership. For example, 'Daniel from Group X is brilliant'. These groups are completely fictitious and have no relation to any real-world group. After encountering multiple students from various groups, you will be asked to make judgement about their groups and allocate new students into groups.

The purpose of this experiment is to study basic processes of learning. There are no foreseeable risks or discomfort associated with completing this study.

You are free to withdraw at any time during the study, any completed tasks until the point of leaving will be deleted and destroyed. You are also free to withdraw your data at any time up until the point of anonymous publication of these data, which will occur at the time the journal article is to be published. To withdraw during the experiment, simply close your browser tab.
